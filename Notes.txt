Dublin is the capital and largest city of Ireland, situated on the country's east coast at the mouth of the River Liffey. Founded by Vikings, it is a city rich in history, known for its Georgian architecture, vibrant cultural scene, and economic importance as a major center for finance, technology, and tourism. 
History: Founded as a Viking settlement around 930 AD, it became a principal city under Norman rule and later the capital of the Irish Free State in 1922. A significant event was the 1916 Easter Uprising, which took place in the city, with some effects still visible today.
Geography: The River Liffey divides the city into the Northside and the Southside, with the city center straddling the two. The Wicklow Hills are to the south, and Dublin Bay is to the east.
Economy: Dublin is a major economic and commercial hub, with a strong presence in finance, technology (particularly software), and tourism. It is also Ireland's chief port.
